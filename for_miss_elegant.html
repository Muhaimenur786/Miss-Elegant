For Miss Elegant

Hey you,

You always had that quiet magic — the kind that walks into a room and fills it with softness without making a sound. I call you "Miss Elegant" not just for how you move, but for how your heart seems to hold galaxies.

I don’t know what the future holds. But I do believe in the power of dua — in that sacred whisper between the broken heart and the One who hears all. Even when everything seems impossible, I still believe. Because... He is Ar-Rahman, Al-Mujib.

You see, this isn’t just about feelings. It’s about faith. About placing my heart in the safest place I know — His hands.

I make dua for you. Not just because I adore you. But because I care. Deeply. Softly. Unshakably. I ask for your peace, your strength, your nearness to Him — even when I’m not near.

I never asked you to wait for me. Or love me back. I only asked Allah. And I left my heart with Him.

Even if you don’t reply — just smile. Somewhere, someone is still raising their hands in sujood... and you're there in that prayer.

Something I once wrote for my future self:

"How can I not believe if He is Ar-Rahman?
How can I not trust if He is Al-Mujib?
Allah accepted the dua of Iblis after his disobedience…
How could He not accept the tears of someone who calls upon Him in the night,
with nothing but a broken heart and unshakable hope?"

“This page isn’t a love letter — it’s a prayer in disguise.”
